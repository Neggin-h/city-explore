Why Florence?

Florence, city of wonders

This is a city that’s renowned for its beauty and history. It is home to famous points of interest such as the Duomo, Pitti Palace and Boboli Gardens, and the Basilica of Santa Maria Novella. But did you know the city has much more to offer than just its main attractions? In fact, several iconic streets in Florence deserve your attention if you’re planning on visiting this fantastic place.

Arguably one of the most beautiful cities in Italy, Florence is full of incredible architecture, great food and historic significance that will keep you going for days. In fact, there are so many things to do in Florence that it is almost certain you will need more than just one visit. There are a lot of beautiful streetscapes, beautiful buildings, and beautiful views of the hills above much of the historic centre. Additionally, there are many fascinating old streets, lined with magnificent ancient palaces built by some of the most interesting families that ever lived.

Best places

Which one is the best in Florence?

Antica Torre di Via Tornabuoni

Housed in a medieval tower on the Via Tornabuoni, and upscale shopping street, this chic boutique hotel next to the River Arno is 5 minutes on foot from the Ponte Vecchio bridge and a 6-minute walk from the Uffizi Gallery. The refined rooms with wood floors and Renaissance style furnishings provide free Wi-Fi, flat-screen TVs and minibars.

Address:

Via de' Tornabuoni, 1, 50123 Firenze FI, Italy

Trattoria Antico Fattore

A historical cenacle of famous painters, writers and lovers of the Tuscan food and wine tradition, one of the most historic trattorias in Florence, L'Antico Fattore has been handing down the recipes of the best dishes of Florentine and Tuscan cuisine since 1865.

Address:

Via Lambertesca 1/3r - 50122 Florence

My sugar gelato

Much of what My Sugar makes comes from ingredients sourced directly from San Lorenzo, the famous market nearby. The menu here is creative and the gelato is made daily onsite. Favorites include melacotta (ricotta, apple, cinnamon, and honey) and Malaga (sweet marsala wine and raisins in a cream base). There are Asian-inspired flavors, too, including black sesame and adzuki bean. Dairy-free vegan options include orange-and-ginger sorbet and almond-milk ice.

Adress:

Via De’Ginori 49r - 50123 Florence
